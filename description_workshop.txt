Eternia is an RPG overhaul for tModLoader built around Soul-bound class identity.

Core loop:
- Begin with an Empty Soul, then bind it to Warrior, Mage, Ranger or Summoner.
- Level through combat EXP while a class Soul is active.
- Spend stat points and passive points to shape your build.
- After the Wall of Flesh, your dominant passive affinity decides your promotion.

Twelve subclasses, each with its own mechanic and full arsenal:
- Warrior: Fighter (Combo), Swordsman (Crimson Trail), Guardian (the Aura).
- Mage: Elementalist (Affinity), Cursed Mage (Corruption), Necromancer (Reserved Life).
- Ranger: Energy Gunner (Temperature), Archer (Concentration), Gunner (Momentum).
- Summoner: Beast Tamer (Ferocity), Advanced Summoner (Legion), Tech Summoner (Power Core).
Each subclass earns its power differently -- taming beasts, fabricating drones, fusing your own
summons, landing perfect shots, and more.

More than progression:
- The Awakening: a real ceremony when the Wall of Flesh grants your subclass.
- Prototype-01 and Prototype-02: Eternia's own bosses, three-phase weapon-module machines with a
  Soul core that ruptures as they break.
- The Boss Codex: a bestiary that tracks your kills, fastest clears, highest rarity faced, and
  every boss's real drops and drop rates.
- Class potions and subclass mechanic tonics, plus Soul Ascension -- a permanent, respec-proof way
  to strengthen your Soul itself.

Soul rules are strict by design: using the wrong weapon class while bound to a class Soul is heavily
punished, and owning a class Soul without keeping one active weakens the player.

This is an in-development build. There is placeholder art throughout and balance is still being
tuned, but the foundation, class progression and vertical slice are playable.
